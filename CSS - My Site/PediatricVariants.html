Pediatric EEG

The EEG of 6 month infants has a posterior dominant rhythm of 4-5 Hz. The PDR should be 8 by age 3. By the age of 10, the PDR should be 10 Hz.

Variants in Pediatric EEG include:

  • Mu
  • Lambda (can be differentiated from epileptic activity by asking the child to close their eyes and see if it disappears)
  • 14 & 6 positive bursts
  • Vertex Waves (in sleep)
  • Sleep Spindles (stage 2 sleep)
  • K complexes (stage 2 sleep)
ACNS Guideline 2 covers the minimum technical standards for Pediatric Encephalography.
